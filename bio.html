About Me :)

Background

I have lived in a small town called Cornwall, NY my whole life. My mom grew up in this town, as well as my grandmother and her parents. I was, as some people call it, and "accident", but I certainly was meant to be on this earth for one reason: to be a computer programmer and an engineer! But seriously, my mom never regretted me one bit. I didn't live with my dad, who lived in Florida for the duration of my childhood. I saw him a good amount and we had a good relationship. My grandmother (who is also my neighbor and is the supplier of whatever we don't have in the house) is like my mom. I lived at her house growing up, and she is truly my best friend.

Interests

Growing up, I had an intense interest in the arts. I loved singing, dancing, and playing the violin and piano. I started singing as soon as I could talk, and I took and still take singing lessons to better my skills. I have danced since I was 3 years old, and danced up until I graduated high school. I met some of my best friends through this amazing experience. On the other hand, I had a vast love for science and math; and I met some of my other best friends through these classes.

College

And now I am gratefully attending Tufts University, and I couldn't be more proud to be able to say I got into this school. I love everyone I've met here, and they continue to push me to better myself and be the best version of myself I can be. I had a hard time figuring out what I wanted to do with my life, since I am so artistic and also into STEM subjects. After being torn between a bunch of different options (and yes, being a singer was an option) and taking some awesome classes in Computer Science, Mechanical Engineering, Architecture, and general science classes, I came to the decision of double majoring in Human Factors Engineering and Computer Science! I haven't regretted it since, even though it is a lot of work. I enjoy and appreciate everyday I have in college and I wouldn't change anything!
